Meditations

A guide to becoming better on all fronts.

"You have power over your mind - not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."

The 6 Core Teachings

1. Master Your Focus

The Dichotomy of Control: Separate what you can control from what you cannot. You cannot control others' opinions, the economy, or the past. You can control your actions, judgments, and character.

Application: Stop leaking energy by complaining. Ask yourself, "Is this up to me?" If not, let it go and focus 100% on your own response.

2. Master Your Mind

The Power of Perception: Things do not upset us; our judgments about those things upset us. An event is just an event. It is your brain that labels it "good" or "bad."

Application: Reframe setbacks. Choose to view obstacles as training. "What stands in the way becomes the way."

3. Master Your Actions

Duty and Discipline: Marcus argued with himself about getting out of bed, reminding himself he was created to do the work of a human being, not to huddle under blankets.

Application: Stop waiting for motivation. Rely on duty and discipline. Show up because it is the right thing to do.

4. Master Your Relationships

Dealing with Difficult People: Marcus reminded himself daily that he would encounter ungrateful and arrogant people, but recognized they act out of ignorance.

Application: Do not let toxic behavior degrade your character. "Be tolerant with others and strict with yourself."

5. Master Your Character

The Four Virtues: Wealth and fame are indifferent. The only true good is Virtue, comprising four pillars:

  • Wisdom: Navigating situations logically.
  • Courage: Doing what is right, even when hard.
  • Justice: Working for the common good.
  • Temperance: Self-discipline and moderation.

6. Master Your Perspective

Memento Mori: "Remember you must die." This wasn't morbid for Marcus; it was a tool for supreme clarity.

Application: Let the reality of your mortality destroy procrastination and petty anxieties. Live fully today.

22 Stoic Concepts for Daily Life

1. Encounter Unkindness

How he dealt with it

He mentally prepared himself every morning to meet difficult people. He treated them with patience, realizing they act out of ignorance, and refused to let their behavior corrupt his own character.

2. Interpretation is Everything

How he dealt with it

If something bad happened, he chose to reject the feeling of being harmed. "Choose not to be harmed, and you won't feel harmed. Don't feel harmed, and you haven't been."

3. Mind Superior to Body

How he dealt with it

When sick or in pain, he did not complain. When tempted, he used logic to override bodily impulses, reminding himself that yielding to base desires is beneath human dignity.

4. Take Deliberate Actions

How he dealt with it

He avoided autopilot by acting as if every single action could be his last, eliminating aimlessness, distraction, and doing things just for the sake of it.

5. Don’t Retreat from the World

How he dealt with it

Even as an exhausted emperor, he didn't isolate himself. He engaged with society, helped others, and fulfilled his civic duties, believing humans are made for cooperation.

6. Value Your Own Opinion

How he dealt with it

He stopped caring what the public thought. He found it strange that we love ourselves more than others, yet value their opinions of us more than our own.

7. Be Open to Correction

How he dealt with it

If someone proved him wrong, he changed his mind gladly, believing that being corrected frees you from ignorance, whereas clinging to false beliefs harms you.

8. Cherish Freedom for All

How he dealt with it

Despite having absolute power, he ruled to protect the common good, treating others fairly and not using people as pawns for tyrannical ends.

9. Have Self Respect

How he dealt with it

He refused to degrade himself by throwing tantrums or obsessing over trivial things, holding himself to the dignified standard of a rational human being.

10. Avoid Complaining

How he dealt with it

"Don't be overheard complaining... Not even to yourself." He accepted reality as it was and focused his energy purely on fixing the problem.

11. The Obstacle is the Way

How he dealt with it

He used challenges as raw material to practice virtues. A betrayal wasn't a roadblock; it became an opportunity to practice forgiveness and grow.

12. Adversity is Part of Nature

How he dealt with it

He expected bad things to happen as a normal part of the changing seasons of life, accepting them with a calm mind rather than being shocked or angry.

13. Adversity Makes Us Stronger

How he dealt with it

He welcomed tough times as a test. Just as a fire burns brighter when things are thrown into it, he used the heavy burdens of warfare to build his resilience.

14. Everything Happened Before

How he dealt with it

When faced with a crisis, he zoomed out, realizing past emperors faced the exact same problems, which gave him immense peace of mind.

15. Deal with What’s in Front of You

How he dealt with it

He confined his attention to the present moment, taking the very next logical step required rather than being overwhelmed by a massive future problem.

16. Focus on Doing What is Right

How he dealt with it

His moral compass dictated his actions. He did the right thing regardless of the political consequences, ease, or the public pushback he received.

17. Despise Cowardice

How he dealt with it

When he didn't want to leave his warm bed on cold mornings, he argued with himself about human duty, despising laziness and acting with courage.

18. The Time for Action is Now

How he dealt with it

He used the reality of death to destroy procrastination, telling himself he could leave life right now, and stopped waiting for tomorrow to be a good man.

19. Get Back on Track

How he dealt with it

When he messed up or lost his temper, he didn't spiral into self-pity. He acknowledged the slip-up and cheerfully returned to his philosophical rhythm.

20. Look Beneath the Surface

How he dealt with it

He aggressively stripped away illusions to destroy superficial desires, describing vintage wine as fermented grapes and royal robes as sheep's wool.

21. Wealth is Neither Good nor Evil

How he dealt with it

He enjoyed his vast wealth but wouldn't mourn it if it vanished, knowing money didn't make him a better person—only internal virtue could do that.

22. Express Gratitude

How he dealt with it

He dedicated Book 1 of Meditations entirely to listing out his friends, family, and mentors, expressing deep gratitude for the specific lessons they taught him.
